Game Analysis

Game Analysis: Super MarioKart (1992)

Look and listen in the game

The music and in-game options give the game an illusion of being in a Mario Kart world. The features allow users to choose a character and one of three racing maps.

Simplicity and Complexity of Settings

The settings are pretty basic with not much depth to them.

Character Control and Interaction

You can control any of the Super Mario Kart characters, including Mario, Princess Peach, Bowser, Toad, Yoshi, and Donkey Kong. Their designs are distinctive and noticeable. There aren't many interactions with non-playable characters (NPCs) as they primarily just race alongside you.

Game Challenge

The main challenge is to beat the other characters to get first place. The player chooses a character and a kart and then tries to finish in the best position.

Interacting with Objects

Objects in the game include a mystery box that provides power-ups to use against other players.

Music and Sounds

The music and sounds are great, featuring classic Mario music that is easily recognizable. The sounds when rounding a corner or hitting a mystery box are very cool and effective.

Real-world Reflection

The game doesn't have much of a real-world response or commentary. The racing courses have grass, blue skies, and flowers, but they are very cartoon-ish, as expected.

Future Development

In the future, the graphics and game mechanics could be improved to be smoother.
